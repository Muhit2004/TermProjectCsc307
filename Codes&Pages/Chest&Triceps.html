Chest & Triceps Training

Build powerful pushing muscles with targeted exercises

Chest Exercises

Bench Press

  • Primary muscle: Pectoralis Major
  • Secondary: Anterior Deltoids, Triceps
  • Sets: 3-4 | Reps: 6-10
  • Rest: 2-3 minutes between sets

The king of chest exercises. Lie flat on bench, grip bar slightly wider than shoulders, lower to chest with control, then press up explosively.

Push-Ups

  • Primary muscle: Pectoralis Major
  • Secondary: Triceps, Core, Shoulders
  • Sets: 3-4 | Reps: 15-25
  • Rest: 1-2 minutes between sets

Classic bodyweight exercise. Keep body straight, hands slightly wider than shoulders, lower chest to ground, push back up.

Incline Dumbbell Press

  • Primary muscle: Upper Pectoralis
  • Secondary: Anterior Deltoids, Triceps
  • Sets: 3-4 | Reps: 8-12
  • Rest: 2-3 minutes between sets

Target upper chest on 30-45° incline. Press dumbbells from chest level, squeezing at the top. Control the descent.

Chest Dips

  • Primary muscle: Lower Pectoralis
  • Secondary: Triceps, Anterior Deltoids
  • Sets: 3-4 | Reps: 8-15
  • Rest: 2-3 minutes between sets

Lean forward slightly, lower body until shoulders are below elbows, press back up. Great for lower chest development.

Triceps Exercises

Tricep Pushdown (Cable)

  • Primary muscle:Triceps Brachii (All three heads)
  • Secondary:None (Isolation exercise)
  • Sets: 3-4 | Reps: 10–15
  • Rest: 60–90 seconds between sets

Stand facing a cable machine with a straight bar, rope, or V-bar attachment. Grip the attachment with hands shoulder-width apart (palms down for bar, neutral for rope). Keep elbows locked at your sides and bend slightly (don’t let them flare). Push the bar/rope down until arms are fully extended, then squeeze triceps at the bottom. Control the weight back up to 90 degrees (forearms parallel to the floor).

Close-Grip Bench Press

  • Primary muscle: Triceps Brachii
  • Secondary: Pectoralis, Anterior Deltoids
  • Sets: 3-4 | Reps: 8-12
  • Rest: 2-3 minutes between sets

Grip bar with hands closer than shoulder-width. Keep elbows tucked, focus on triceps engagement throughout the movement.

Overhead Tricep Extension

  • Primary muscle: Triceps Brachii (Long Head)
  • Secondary: Core for stability
  • Sets: 3-4 | Reps: 10-15
  • Rest: 2 minutes between sets

Hold dumbbell overhead, lower behind head by bending elbows, extend back up. Keep elbows stationary throughout.

Diamond Push-ups

  • Primary muscle: Triceps Brachii
  • Secondary: Inner Chest, Core
  • Sets: 3-4 | Reps: 8-15
  • Rest: 1-2 minutes between sets

Form diamond shape with hands, keep elbows close to body, lower chest to hands, push back up. Advanced bodyweight exercise.

Training Tips for Chest & Triceps

  • Always warm up with light cardio and dynamic stretching
  • Focus on proper form over heavy weight
  • Control the negative (lowering) portion of each rep
  • Allow 48-72 hours rest between chest/tricep sessions
  • Progress gradually by adding weight or reps weekly
  • Include both compound and isolation exercises
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your muscles with proper nutrition
