Full-Body Plan

5-Day Full Body High Frequency Routine

  • Frequency: 5 days per week (consecutive or with 1-2 rest days)
  • Duration: 6+ weeks (long-term program)
  • Focus: Maximizing muscle protein synthesis, advanced hypertrophy
  • Rest: 90-120 seconds between sets

Based on the Norwegian Frequency Project, this advanced routine hits each muscle group nearly daily with optimized volume distribution. Perfect for experienced lifters looking to break plateaus through frequent, high-quality stimulation.

  • Click here For The Science Behind It

Day 1: Upper Body Strength

  • Bench Press: 4 sets x 6-8 reps
  • Pull-Ups: 4 sets x 6-10 reps
  • Overhead Press: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Barbell Rows: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Dips: 3 sets x 10-12 reps

Focus on progressive overload in compound movements. Start with RPE 7-8 for first 2 weeks, then increase intensity. Maintain strict form to avoid fatigue accumulation.

Day 2: Lower Body Power

  • Squats: 4 sets x 6-8 reps
  • Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Leg Press: 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Calf Raises: 4 sets x 12-15 reps

Prioritize explosive concentric movements with controlled eccentrics. The low exercise count per session allows maximum effort on each lift.

Day 3: Push/Pull Balance

  • Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Lateral Raises: 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Face Pulls: 3 sets x 12-15 reps

Hypertrophy-focused day with moderate weights and higher reps. Ideal for addressing weak points while maintaining movement quality.

Day 4: Full Body Strength

  • Deadlifts: 3 sets 5 reps
  • Front Squats: 3 sets 6 reps
  • Weighted Dips: 3 sets 8 reps
  • Barbell Curls: 3 sets 10 reps
  • Hanging Leg Raises: 3 sets 12 reps

Low-rep deadlifts prime the CNS. Front squats complement back squats from Day 2 without overloading the spine.

Day 5: Volume & Weak Points

  • Dumbbell Bench Press: 4 sets 10-12 reps
  • T-Bar Rows: 3 sets 10-12 reps
  • Leg Curls: 3 sets 12-15 reps
  • Overhead Triceps: 3 sets 12 reps
  • Plank Variations: 3 sets 30-45 sec

Address individual weak points. The DB bench allows for greater range of motion than barbell variations.

Upper/Lower Split Plan

4-Day Upper/Lower Hypertrophy (Beginner-Friendly)

  • Frequency: 4 days/week (2 upper, 2 lower)
  • Duration: 6-12 weeks (ideal for beginners)
  • Focus: Strength + hypertrophy with progressive overload
  • Rest: 60-90s for isolation, 2-3min for compounds

Modified upper/lower split by Dr. Swole (MD, physique athlete). Uses top-set back-off method for heavy lifts and strategically moves biceps/delts to lower days for fresh training. Perfect for beginners prioritizing form and steady progression.

Upper Body Day 1 (Chest/Back Focus)

  • Bench Press: 4 sets (1 top set 5-8 reps + 3 back-off sets)
  • Single-Arm DB Rows: 3x6-10
  • Machine Flies: 3x10-15
  • Reverse Lat Pulldowns: 3x10-15
  • Cable Pressdowns: 3x10-15 (Triceps)
  • Upright Rows + Lateral Raises: 2x6-12 (Delts)

Key Notes: Superset rows with bench press if short on time. Top-set back-off method builds strength while accumulating volume.

Lower Body Day 1 (Quads/Hams)

  • Squats: 4 sets (1 top set 5-8 reps + 3 back-off sets)
  • Romanian Deadlifts: 2x6-10
  • Lunges: 3x8-12/leg
  • Leg Extensions: 2x10-15
  • Hammer Curls + Cable Curls: 2x6-12 (Biceps)
  • Single-Leg Calf Raises: 3x8-12

Key Notes: Biceps moved to lower day for fresh training. RDLs are highly stimulative keep reps controlled.

Upper Body Day 2 (Shoulders/Back)

  • Overhead Press: 3 sets (1 top set 5-8 reps + 2 back-off sets)
  • Weighted Chin-Ups: 3x6-10
  • Close-Grip Bench: 3x6-10 (Triceps/Chest)
  • Chest-Supported Rows: 3x8-12
  • DB Skull Crushers: 3x8-12
  • Upright Rows + Lateral Raises: 2x8-20 (Delts)

Key Notes: Close-grip bench emphasizes triceps. Vary rep ranges for side delts (e.g., 8-12 one day, 12-20 next).

Lower Body Day 2 (Posterior Chain)

  • Deadlifts: 3 sets (1 top set 5-8 reps + 2 back-off sets)
  • Smith Machine Squats: 3x8-12
  • Leg Press: 3x8-12
  • Leg Curls: 2x10-15
  • Incline + Preacher Curls: 2x6-15 (Biceps)
  • Calf Raises: 3x10-15 (superset with leg press)

Key Notes: Deadlifts prioritized early. Biceps again trained fresh. Smith squats reduce fatigue for beginners.

Recommended Weekly Schedule

  • Day 1: Upper 1
  • Day 2: Rest
  • Day 3: Lower 1
  • Day 4: Rest
  • Day 5: Upper 2
  • Day 6: Lower 2
  • Day 7: Rest

Flexibility: Can run Upper/Lower back-to-back if needed (e.g., Upper 1 → Lower 1 → Rest → Upper 2 → Lower 2). Avoid repeating muscle groups within 48hrs.

Push-Pull-Legs (PPL) Powerbuilding Split

6-Day PPL Hypertrophy/Strength Split

  • Frequency: 6 days/week (Push, Pull, Legs x 2)
  • Duration: 6-12 weeks (part of Powerbuilding 2.0 program)
  • Focus: Hypertrophy with strength foundations
  • Rest: 2-3min for compounds, 60-90s for isolations

Designed by Jeff Nippard, this PPL split alternates between strength-focused (lower reps) and hypertrophy-focused (higher reps) days. Includes advanced techniques like drop sets, eccentric accents, and mechanical drop sets for growth.

Day 1: Legs (Quad Focused)

  • Squats: 3x4 80% 1RM (RPE 5-8)
  • Romanian Deadlifts: 3x10-12 (light in Week 1)
  • Unilateral Leg Press: 3x15/leg (RPE 8-9)
  • Eccentric Leg Extensions: 3x10-12 (3-4s lowering)
  • Seated Hamstring Curls: 3x10-12 + drop set last set
  • Standing Calf Raises: 3x12-15 + extra reps for weak side
  • Core: Weighted Decline Crunches + L-Sit Planks

Key Notes: Squats use "knees out" technique for hip engagement. Unilateral leg press pushed close to failure. Rectus femoris targeted via seat-adjusted leg extensions.

Day 2: Push (Chest Focused)

  • Bench Press: 3x8 72.5% 1RM
  • Machine Shoulder Press: 3x12
  • Dips: 3x12-15 (full ROM stretch)
  • Eccentric Skull Crushers: 3x8-10 (3s lowering)
  • Cable Lateral Raises: 3x12 + myo-reps last set
  • Cable Tricep Kickbacks: 2x20-30 (1s up/1s down)

Key Notes: Bench uses slight pause. Dips emphasize pec stretch. Lateral raises use progressive ROM reduction (21s method).

Day 3: Pull (Lat Focused)

  • Weighted Pull-Ups: 3x6 (upright posture)
  • Seated Cable Rows: 3x10-12 (elbows tucked)
  • Kneeling Cable Pullovers: 3xAMRAP (1s tempo)
  • Hammer Curls: 3x10-12 (looser form)
  • Incline DB Curls: 3x10-12 (strict, supinated)

Key Notes: Pull-ups prioritize shoulder adduction. Rows use MAG grip. Pullovers maintain constant tension.

Day 4: Legs (Posterior Chain)

  • Deadlifts: 3x3 80-85% 1RM
  • Hack Squats: 3x10-12 (knees forward)
  • Unilateral Hip Thrusts: 3x10-12 (glute squeeze)
  • Nordic Ham Curls + Prisoner Back Extensions: 3x10-12
  • Unilateral Calf Raises: 3x10-12
  • Core: Weighted L-Sit Holds

Key Notes: Deadlifts emphasize bar path. Hack squats mimic barbell stance. Hip thrusts use posterior pelvic tilt.

Day 5: Push (Delt Focused)

  • Overhead Press: 4x4 80% 1RM
  • Close-Grip Bench: 3x10 (hands-width grip)
  • Low-to-High Cable Flys: 3x10-12 + drop set
  • Overhead Tricep Extensions: 3x10-12
  • DB Lateral Raise 21s: 3x7 full + 7 top + 7 bottom
  • Neck Work: 8-way machine

Key Notes: OHP uses arched upper back. Lateral raises use progressive ROM. Cable flys target upper pecs.

Day 6: Pull (Mid-Back Focused)

  • Omni-Grip Lat Pulldowns: 3x6 (wide → close grip)
  • Chest-Supported Rows: 3x10-12 (scapular protraction)
  • Rope Face Pulls: 3x12-15 (rear delt focus)
  • Incline DB Shrugs: 3x10-12 (optional, with straps)
  • Rear Delt Flys: 2x15 + 10-15 (scapular variation)
  • EZ Bar Curls: 3x10-12 (pronated → supinated)

Key Notes: Rows exaggerate stretch. Face pulls rotate externally. Curls pre-fatigue forearms for biceps focus.

Recommended Weekly Schedule

  • Day 1: Legs (Quads)
  • Day 2: Push (Chest)
  • Day 3: Pull (Lats)
  • Day 4: Legs (Posterior)
  • Day 5: Push (Delts)
  • Day 6: Pull (Mid-Back)
  • Day 7: Rest

Note: Part of Powerbuilding 2.0 program—alternates with full-body strength weeks. Adjust volume if running standalone.

Workout Plan Selection Tips

  • Choose a plan that matches your current fitness level
  • Ensure the plan fits your schedule and available time
  • Consider your primary goal (strength, muscle, endurance)
  • Progress gradually through beginner to advanced plans
  • Allow for proper recovery between workouts
  • Track your progress and adjust weights accordingly
  • Change your plan every 8-12 weeks to avoid plateaus
